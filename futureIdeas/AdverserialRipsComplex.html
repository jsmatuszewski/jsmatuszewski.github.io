Adversial Complexes

!!! Idea is under construction !!!

I want to captrue the structure of (at least) two opposing entities sharing the same physical space. I want to use something Čech/Vietoris-Ripsy for each set of points. But the construction would not result in a filtration, because I want to specify rules for deleting simplexes as the filtration paramater grows. I'll explain with an example but be warned, this idea was not born from a compelling use-case.

Example 1: Zombie Apocalypse*

This apocalypse is realistic in the sense that the zombifying-virus is psychological in nature not physiological. The long awaited zombie appocalypse is in full swing. Zombies and normies (non-zombies) both have outposts for communicating. We could compute the persistent homology (with max dimension = 2) on the geospatial points corresponding to only normies to get some sense of the "coverage" of their outposts. We could do the same for the Zombies. Sidenote: a Čech complex maybe makes the most sense here if belonging to a common simplex means direct communication between any 2 outposts is possible. Instead of doing the standard complication (the act of turning points into complexes) we delete an outpost (0-simplex) from the normies network when a zombie outpost is within distance 2r. Maybe because at this value r the deleted outpost is no longer trustworthy.
